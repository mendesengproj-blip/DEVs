Paper III - Task A: dimensional audit (SI, c != 1) and point-mass test
======================================================================

STEP-BY-STEP DIMENSIONAL DERIVATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Pi^(A) = (2/3) kappa (theta')^2     [theta' dimensionless]
   For Pi^(A) to be energy density [kg/(m s^2)]:
       kappa = beta a0^2 / (8 pi G)
       [kappa] = (m/s^2)^2 / (m^3/(kg s^2)) = kg/(m s^2)   OK

2. Linearized Einstein slip:
       nabla^2 (Psi - Phi) = (8 pi G / c^2) Pi^(A)
   [LHS] = (m^2/s^2)/m^2 = 1/s^2
   [RHS] = m^3/(kg s^2) * 1/(m^2/s^2) * kg/(m s^2)
         = m^3 kg^-1 s^-2 * s^2/m^2 * kg m^-1 s^-2 = 1/s^2  OK

3. Substituting kappa:
       S = (2 beta/3) a0^2 (theta')^2 / c^2
   With (theta')^2 = (g_N / (mu a0))^2 (exact AQUAL spherical):
       S = (2 beta/3) g_N^2 / (mu^2 c^2)
   [S] = (m/s^2)^2 / (m^2/s^2) = 1/s^2   OK

4. Deep-MOND limit mu^2 -> g_N/a0:
       S_dM = (2 beta/3) g_N a0 / c^2
   [S_dM] = (m/s^2)(m/s^2)/(m^2/s^2) = 1/s^2   OK

CONCLUSION ON UNITS
   The c^2 factor from the linearized Einstein equation
   was hidden in the c=1 derivation of Paper I.  Restoring
   it gives the dimensionally consistent source above.

INTERPRETATION
   S_correct (now dimensionally clean) scales as g_N a0 / c^2 ~ 1/r^2
   in deep-MOND.  But the slip formula of Paper I, eta-1 ~ r sqrt(a0/GM),
   grows linearly with r and therefore demands a source S_req ~ a0/r.
   These two functional forms (1/r^2 vs 1/r) are STRUCTURALLY
   incompatible.  Restoring c^2 fixes the units but does NOT
   reconcile the radial dependence.

VERDICT: max residual 3.01e+45 % >> 10 %.
   S_correct (with c^2 and exact AQUAL theta') does NOT reproduce
   the Paper I analytic formula either.  The discrepancy is now
   a factor ~ c^2 r / (GM) - the same 10^7 - 10^8 ratio observed
   in earlier tests.  Per Task B criterion (residual < 10 %),
   Task C (DGSAT-I) is NOT executed.

STRUCTURAL DIAGNOSIS
   The Paper I formula behaves as eta-1 ~ r in deep-MOND, which
   corresponds to source ~ a0/r (or g_obs/r times a constant).
   This is a *long-range* source not produced by any pointwise
   product of g_N, mu, rho_b, a0, c.  A purely local closure of
   the form S = F(g_N, mu, a0)/c^n therefore CANNOT reproduce
   the Paper I deep-MOND scaling.

   Possible resolutions for Paper III (none endorsed by this test):
   (i)  The slip relation comes from an integral kernel, not a
        local Poisson equation - i.e. (Psi-Phi)(r) is a Green's
        function CONVOLUTION over rho_b, not a Laplacian inverse.
   (ii) The field equation contains a non-local operator
        (e.g. AeST disformal sector) whose linearization is
        not nabla^2 but a more complicated kernel.
   (iii) Paper I's eta-1 formula is a phenomenological ansatz
        constrained by lensing/MOND consistency, not a Green-
        function-derivable expression.  The Paper II caveat
        already concedes this - it should be the operative
        position for the published series.
